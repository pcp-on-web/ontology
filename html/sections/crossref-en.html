Academic Documentsc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://pcp-on-web.de/ontology#AcademicDocuments

Refers to academic documents in the narrowest sense. Documents that were relevant to the organisation of everyday university life, such as lecture catalogues or professorial reports. They are distinct from publications such as textbooks (teaching material), which addressed a larger audience of scholars and educated people via the contemporary publication market.
has super-classes
Document c
has sub-classes
Dissertation c, Lecture Catalogue c, Programs and Speeches c, Report c, Statutes c, Textbook c
is in domain of
Academic Year dp, has faculty op, miscellaneous/further information dp

Disputationc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://pcp-on-web.de/ontology#Disputation

Refers to the practice of disputation, a scientific debate which had the purpose of practising curricula, to obtain academic titles and the right to teach and give lectures (venia legendi). It also refers to inaugural disputations of newly vocated professors. The printed medium of a disputation is the dissertation.
has super-classes
Teaching Practices c
